About Me

I’m Kommalapati Lahari, an IIT Patna Computer Science graduate, currently working with STMicroelectronics.

At STMicroelectronics, I drive research at the intersection of agentic frameworks and hardware co-design. My work involves designing end-to-end pipelines for Design Space Optimization (DSO) and Microarchitecture Exploration, utilizing generative AI to optimize accelerator-level C++ code and automate verification. I believe that true efficiency requires unifying algorithmic intelligence with hardware realization.

Previously, I developed InvGuard, a multi-agent AI system for smart contract security that achieved 96.2% invariant precision (mitigating 74/91 real-world exploits) and is currently under review for FSE 2026. During my time at IIT Hyderabad, I built real-time driver behavior analysis pipelines using PCA and K-Means, which I presented at the TRB Conference 2025.

My current research focuses on developing efficient, sustainable AI accelerators and Edge Language Models (ELMs) optimized for memory-constrained deployment. I am actively exploring post-transformer architectures like the Mamba series and state-space models that offer linear-time inference.

Guided by the Roofline model, I target memory access efficiency—utilizing Neural Architecture Search (NAS), quantization, and sparsity to design architectures that retain data locality while sustaining performance. My goal is to enable "deep edge intelligence" where localized, adaptive inference powers the next generation of physical AI and robotics.
